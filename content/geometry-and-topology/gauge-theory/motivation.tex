\begin{dfn}[The Maxwell Equations]
    Let \( \va{E} \), \( \va{B} \), \( \rho \), and \( \va{j} \) denote the electric field, magnetic field, charge density, and current density respectively. In units where the speed of light is equal to \( 1 \), the \vocab{Maxwell Equations} are 
    \begin{align*}
        &\largetriangledown \cdot \va{B} = 0 && \largetriangledown \times \va{E} + \pdv{\va{B}}{t} = 0 \\
        &\largetriangledown \cdot \va{E} = \rho &&\largetriangledown \times \va{B} - \pdv{\va{E}}{t} = \va{j}
    \end{align*}
     If t \( \rho = 0 \) and \( \va{j}= \va{0} \), then we call the above the \vocab{vacuum Maxwell equations}.
\end{dfn}
