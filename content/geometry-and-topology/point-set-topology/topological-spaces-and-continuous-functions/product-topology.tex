\begin{dfn}\label{def:basis-for-product-topology}
    Let \( X \) and \( Y \) be topological spaces. Then the \vocab{product topology \( \mathscr{T}_{X \times Y} \)} on \( X \times Y \) is the topology generated by the basis \( \mathscr{B}_{X \times Y} \), where 
    \[ \mathscr{B}_{X \times Y} = \left\{ U \times V \ \middle| \  U \text{ is open in } X \text{ and } V \text{ is open in } Y. \right\} \]
\end{dfn}

\begin{exercise}
    Check that the basis described above is a basis.
\end{exercise}
\begin{solution}
    For the first condition of a basis, we just take \( X \) and \( Y \) to be our open sets. \\ 
    For the second condition, suppose that we are given basis elements \( U_{1} \times  V_{1} \) and \( U_{2} \times  V_{2} \). Since 
    \[ \left( U_{1} \times  V_{1}\right) \cap \left( U_{2} \times  V_{2}\right) = \left( U_{1} \cap U_{2} \right) \times \left( V_{1} \cap V_{2} \right)\]
    The second condition holds by the finite intersection property of the topology.
\end{solution}

\begin{lemma}
    Let \( \mathscr{T}_{X \times  Y} \) be the topology generated by the basis as described in \cref{def:basis-for-product-topology}. If \( \mathscr{B}_{X} \) and \( \mathscr{B}_{Y} \) are bases for the topologies on \( X \) and \( Y \), respectively. Then 
    \[ \mathscr{B}_{X} \times  \mathscr{B}_{Y} = \left\{ B_{X} \times  B_{Y} \ \ \middle| \ \ B_{X} \in \mathscr{B}_{X},\ B_{Y} \in \mathscr{B}_{Y} \right\} \]
    is a basis for the product topology.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  We will apply \cref{thm:open-cover-collection-basis-characterization}. Pick an arbitrary open set \( Z \in \mathscr{T}_{X \times Y} \) and \( \left( a,b \right) \in Z \). By definition of a basis, there exists open sets \( U_{a} \) and \( V_{b} \) for which \( \left( a,b \right) \in U_{a} \times V_{b} \subseteq Z \) In particular, \( a \in U_{a} \) and \( b \in V_{b} \). Since \( \mathscr{B}_{X} \) and \( \mathscr{B}_{Y} \) are bases for \( X \) and \( Y \) respectively. There exists \( B_{a} \in \mathscr{B}_{X}  \) and \( B_{b} \in \mathscr{B}_{Y} \) for which \( a \in B_{a} \subseteq U_{a} \) and \( b \in B_{b} \subseteq V_{b} \). So \( \left( a,b \right) \in B_{a} \times B_{b} \subseteq U_{a} \times V_{b} \subseteq Z \). Hence, \( \mathscr{B}_{X} \times  \mathscr{B}_{Y} \) is a basis for the product topology on \( X \times Y \) by \cref{thm:open-cover-collection-basis-characterization}.
\end{proof}
